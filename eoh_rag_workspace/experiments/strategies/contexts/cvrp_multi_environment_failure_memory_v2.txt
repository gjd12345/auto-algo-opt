AGENT MEMORY — development and fresh-diagnostic evidence, not held-out evidence.

Preserve the confirmed n2 backbone unless a structural alternative improves both the fixed search batch and the independent confirmation batch.

Two plausible-looking structures have already failed on new generated instances:

1. A local-density bonus improved uniform 50 and clustered 100 but regressed on rectangular 200; it failed with 44 wins and 46 losses.
2. A remaining-demand variance penalty passed the fixed search and confirmation objectives, but then failed a disjoint 90-instance diagnostic with 18 wins, 23 losses, 49 ties, and mean improvement -0.066%. Only uniform 50 had a positive mean.

Do not reuse density bonuses, remaining-demand variance penalties, or coefficient-only repairs of these ideas. Use the per-environment search and confirmation objectives to propose a meaningfully different routing structure that can operate across square, clustered, and rectangular geometries. Avoid trading a clear regression in one environment for a tiny aggregate gain.
